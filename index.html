Smart packing tips

Pack light, travel greenPack outfits you can mix & match. Less weight = lower carbon footprint on flights.
Roll, don't foldRolling clothes saves space and reduces wrinkles. Use packing cubes for better organization.
Valuables checklistAlways carry passport, travel insurance, emergency contacts, and copies in a separate bag.

Eco travel tips

Reusable bottle & bagBring a reusable water bottle to cut down on single-use plastic at your destination.
Walk or cycle locallyUse bikes, walking, or public transport instead of taxis when exploring cities.
Choose eco-certified staysLook for hotels with Green Key or EarthCheck certification — they monitor water and energy use.
Buy local, eat localSupport local artisans and restaurants over international chains. Better for economy and environment.

Money saving tips

Book flights midweekTuesday and Wednesday flights are typically 10–15% cheaper than weekend departures.
Use local currencyAlways pay in local currency to avoid dynamic currency conversion fees.
Eat where locals eatRestaurants one block off the main tourist street offer better food at half the price.
Get a local SIM cardA local prepaid SIM is nearly always cheaper than international roaming with your home carrier.

Safety tips

Check travel advisoriesAlways check your government's travel advisories before booking international trips.
Get travel insuranceMedical emergencies abroad can be very expensive. A basic policy costs less than one doctor visit.
Hotel room securityUse the deadbolt and door latch. Keep valuables in the room safe, not in your bag.

Cultural tips

Learn basic phrasesEven "hello", "thank you", and "sorry" in the local language can make a big difference.
Dress appropriatelyResearch dress codes for temples, mosques, and local customs before packing.
Ask before photographingAlways ask permission before photographing people, especially in religious or rural settings.
